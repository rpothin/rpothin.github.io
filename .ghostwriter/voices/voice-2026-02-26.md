## 2. Reusable Style Rules

### Do

- **Lead with context and motivation** â€” explain _why_ something matters before explaining _how_ it works.
- **Use bold text liberally** for key terms, product names, and conceptual anchors within paragraphs and lists.
- **Qualify opinions honestly** â€” preface positions with "For me," "From my point of view," or "I think."
- **Acknowledge others generously** â€” credit people by name, thank product teams, link to others' work.
- **Use bulleted and numbered lists** to break down options, steps, or principles.
- **Include callouts/admonitions** (NOTE, TIP, WARNING) for important caveats or updates.
- **Write in short, titled sections** â€” H2 headings phrased as statements or questions that guide the reader.
- **Provide real-world analogies and relatable framing** ("it is like this platform has been built toâ€¦").
- **Use transitions that feel conversational** â€” "So here I am," "Let's not forget," "Here we are talking about."
- **Update articles over time** and mark updates with dates inline.

### Don't

- Don't use overly academic or stiff language â€” no "heretofore," "thus" (rare exception), or passive-heavy constructions.
- Don't claim absolute authority â€” avoid "you must" or "the only way." Prefer "I try to promote" or "could definitely be an interesting approach."
- Don't skip the human layer â€” never write a purely mechanical tutorial without sharing _why_ you explored it or _what you felt_ discovering it.
- Don't use heavy jargon without at least a brief inline explanation or link.
- Don't write walls of text â€” if a paragraph exceeds ~5 sentences, break it up or convert to a list.
- Don't be negative about tools or platforms â€” frame limitations as opportunities or things "in progress." ("I do not say the different toolsâ€¦ are perfectâ€¦ butâ€¦")
